\abstract{\textbf{Motivation:}
Gene-regulatory-network (GRN) inference from single-cell transcriptomics is
increasingly used to prioritize regulatory hypotheses, yet most benchmarks
evaluate accuracy under fixed preprocessing without testing whether rankings
survive realistic analytical perturbations, and most published fragility claims
are made under a single scorer family.\\
\textbf{Results:}
We audit GRN edge rankings across five fragility axes---cell count,
clustering resolution, rare-cell reliability, donor generalization, and
integration sensitivity---on four Tabula Sapiens tissues, one
non-Tabula-Sapiens external lung cohort (Travaglini et al.\ 2020
SmartSeq2), and one disease cohort (Delorey et al.\ 2021 COVID-19
lung atlas, 116{,}313 cells, 27 donors), using five scorer families:
Pearson correlation, mutual information, GRNBoost2, GENIE3, and
scGPT attention.
Our central findings are that:
(i)~cluster-resolution stability under a bounded-drift
Resolution-Sensitivity-Score sits 9--13 standard deviations below a
rank-permutation null, yet every cohort fails a within-coarse
constrained shuffle, so dual-null calibration recommends coarse or
hybrid clustering across the full 66-point weight simplex;
(ii)~the rare-vs-abundant reliability-gate gap direction is preserved
at 100\% of 5{,}880 reasonable threshold combinations, establishing
that the 0\% rare passage rate is not an artifact of threshold choice;
(iii)~the minimum valid cell count (MVCC) is strongly scorer-dependent:
under scGPT attention it exceeds 3{,}000 cells, whereas under
Pearson-over-highly-variable-genes it is 200 cells for four of six
cohorts and 500 cells for the two largest ones (primary immune and
Delorey COVID lung), and it is anchor-invariant;
(iv)~6--8 donors are needed for immune generalization under strict
cells-per-donor balancing, and fewer under looser balancing;
(v)~integration method choice is not neutral: Harmony preserves
rankings reasonably (Spearman 0.63--0.87, sign-flip 6--17\%), scVI
moderately perturbs them (Spearman 0.36--0.57, sign-flip 18--30\%),
and Scanorama on kidney and lung produces near-random rankings
(Spearman $\approx 0$, sign-flip 44--47\%);
(vi)~scGPT attention rankings are statistically uncorrelated with
classical-scorer rankings on matched kidney cells (Spearman
$\rho \leq 0.20$, top-100 Jaccard $\leq 0.06$), so within-scorer
fragility profiles do not transfer across scorer families.
A degree-preserving edge-rewire null is cleared at $p = 0.02$ on every
cohort, rebutting the possibility that the within-coarse-shuffle
failures reflect null over-stringency rather than genuine signal loss.
No single cohort passes all five axes under calibrated criteria.\\

\section{Introduction}

Single-cell RNA-sequencing enables transcriptome-wide inference of
gene regulatory networks (GRNs). Classical correlation- and tree-based
scorers (SCENIC~\citep{Aibar2017}, GRNBoost2~\citep{Moerman2019},
GENIE3~\citep{Huynh-Thu2010}, ARACNe-family methods) and
attention-derived scores from single-cell foundation models such as
scGPT~\citep{Cui2024} all rank TF--target edges as regulatory
hypotheses, but confidence in those rankings depends on analytical
choices that are rarely stress-tested. Published
benchmarks~\citep{Pratapa2020, Chen2018} evaluate accuracy against
fixed references under fixed preprocessing, leaving open the question
of whether the same edges survive a different cell count, clustering
resolution, integration method, or donor cohort. Atlas-level
integration benchmarks~\citep{Luecken2022, Tran2020} document that
batch correction perturbs downstream analyses, but the specific impact
on TF--target ranking stability has not been systematically
characterised.

We address this gap by conducting a systematic multi-axis robustness audit
of TF--target edge rankings across five scorer families (Pearson correlation,
mutual information, GRNBoost2, GENIE3, and scGPT attention) and six cohorts
(four Tabula Sapiens tissues, one external-lab lung dataset, and one
COVID-19 disease atlas). Our audit spans
five axes that correspond to the most common sources of analytical variation
in single-cell GRN workflows:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Cell-count subsampling:} How many cells are needed for stable
      rankings, is that threshold dependent on the reference-anchor cell
      count, and do prior-constrained policies rescue small-sample regimes?
\item \textbf{Cluster-resolution sensitivity:} Does switching between coarse
      and fine clustering change which edges are prioritized, and does the
      conclusion survive rank-permutation nulls, weight-choice sensitivity,
      gene-dimension sweeps, and a triple-null calibration?
\item \textbf{Rare-cell-type reliability:} Do rare cell types yield
      mechanistic edges that pass the same confidence gates as abundant
      types, and how robust is that gap to the choice of gate thresholds?
\item \textbf{Donor generalization:} How many donors are needed for rankings
      to transfer to held-out individuals, and is the threshold dependent
      on cells-per-donor balancing choices?
\item \textbf{Integration-method sensitivity:} Do batch-correction
      methods (Harmony, scVI, and Scanorama) alter edge rankings, and do
      any of those changes improve biological validity as measured by
      perturbation-backed evidence rather than literature precision alone?
\end{enumerate}

Beyond the per-axis questions, we ask an orthogonal one: \emph{do
fragility conclusions drawn under one scorer transfer to another?}
We find that scGPT attention rankings are statistically uncorrelated
with Pearson / mutual information / GRNBoost2 / GENIE3 rankings on the
same cells and the same edge universe ($\rho \leq 0.20$, top-100
Jaccard $\leq 0.06$), which generalises the message from ``rankings
are fragile under preprocessing perturbations'' to ``rankings are
fragile under preprocessing perturbations \emph{and} scorer choice.''

Each axis was developed through an adversarial-review loop in which an
AI-assisted review agent systematically challenged the initial analysis for
methodological weaknesses (confounded null designs, overfitted
hyperparameters, hidden data leakage, uninterpretable composite metrics,
panel-selection arbitrariness), corrective experiments were designed and
executed, and claims were updated accordingly. A full protocol description
(base model, prompt templates, round-by-round logs, and a
pre-registered audit of 27 agent-raised issues under a frozen rubric)
is provided in Supplementary Methods~S2.

The remainder of this paper describes the shared experimental setup
(Section~2), presents per-axis results (Section~3), reports a
scorer-divergence analysis (Section~3.6), analyses cross-axis
interactions (Section~3.7), and provides a practical guidelines table
for practitioners (Section~4).

\section{Methods}

\subsection{Datasets, preprocessing, and panels}

All analyses used processed single-cell datasets from the Tabula
Sapiens atlas~\citep{TabulaSapiens2022}, one external lung cohort
(Travaglini et al.\ 2020 SmartSeq2), and one COVID-19 disease lung
atlas (Delorey et al.\ 2021~\citep{Delorey2021}; Table~\ref{tab:datasets}).
Datasets were preprocessed using Scanpy~\citep{Wolf2018} following established
best practices~\citep{Luecken2019}. The primary normalization was
depth-normalization (``\texttt{normalize\_total}'' with ``\texttt{target\_sum}='' $10^4$)
followed by \texttt{log1p}, with the top 600 highly-variable genes selected
using the Seurat v3 flavor. Two alternative normalization schemes---median-ratio
size-factor normalization and Pearson residuals~\citep{Lause2021}---were
evaluated as an ablation (Supplementary Fig.~S6) and produced the same
calibrated recommendation on every axis we examined. Every pipeline run
stamps the full preprocessing parameter set into its provenance record;
numerical reproducibility is therefore traceable to an explicit
configuration file rather than to narrative prose.

TF--target edge panels used in axes with panel-restricted scoring were
loaded from a shared registry (Supplementary Table~S1) that resolves each
panel to an exact edge list at runtime. Named panels include:
``\emph{primary}'' (DoRothEA~\citep{GarciaAlonso2019} confidence A/B
$\cap$ TRRUST~\citep{Han2018}, 3{,}384 edges before gene-universe
restriction); ``\emph{dorothea\_ab}''; ``\emph{trrust}'';
``\emph{hematopoiesis\_76$\times$108}'' (76 hematopoiesis TFs $\times$ 108
immune / cross-tissue targets = 8{,}208 directed edges, used in
Axis~4); and ``\emph{shared\_36}'' (the TRRUST-intersected
activation/repression panel used for Axis~3 cross-dataset sensitivity).
Axis~2 operates on all HVG pairs rather than a curated panel so that the
coarse-versus-fine question is closed under a shared edge universe.

\subsection{Edge scorers}

Every axis pipeline accepts a pluggable ``edge scorer'' via a shared
interface, so the same preprocessing and null library feeds every
methodological choice. Five scorers are implemented:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Pearson correlation} on column-standardized expression.
      Sign of correlation is tracked separately from the magnitude score.
\item \textbf{Mutual information} using scikit-learn's
      ``\texttt{mutual\_info\_regression}'' (Kraskov $k$-nearest-neighbour
      estimator, $k=3$, seed fixed).
\item \textbf{GRNBoost2}~\citep{Moerman2019} and
      \textbf{GENIE3}~\citep{Huynh-Thu2010}: per-target gradient-boosted
      and random-forest regression importance respectively (500
      estimators, sqrt feature subsampling).
\item \textbf{scGPT attention}~\citep{Cui2024}, extracted from the public
      \emph{whole-human} checkpoint. For each cell we tokenise the panel
      genes, forward-pass through the twelve transformer layers, and
      average attention weights across all layers and heads; edge scores
      are the symmetrised cell-wise mean attention between each TF and
      target token.
\end{itemize}

The scorer used for any figure or statistic is stated in the caption
and §3.6 reports the pairwise agreement matrix. No model is trained or
fine-tuned in this work: scGPT enters strictly as a frozen pretrained
feature extractor, and the remaining scorers are unsupervised
statistical estimators.

\subsection{Metric definitions}

For top-$k$ index sets $T_a^k, T_b^k$ from two scoring runs on a shared
edge universe, the bounded-drift Resolution Sensitivity Score is
$\text{RSS}_k = w_o(1 - \text{overlap}_k) + w_j(1 - \text{Jaccard}_k) + w_d\,\text{drift\_norm}_k$,
where $\text{drift\_norm}_k = \min(1, \operatorname{median}_{i \in T_a^k \cap T_b^k}|r_{a,i} - r_{b,i}|/k)$
maps median rank-shift to $[0, 1]$. Unless noted, $k = 1{,}000$ and
weights are $(0.4, 0.3, 0.3)$; the weight simplex is swept in §3.2.
The remaining metrics are standard: Spearman rank stability,
null-separation AUC, tail gap (top-5\% quantile observed minus null),
sign-flip rate, and mean absolute rank shift. A null family is
\emph{cleared} when the empirical $p$ against it is $\leq 0.05$
with $\geq 19$ permutations.

\subsection{Axis~1: Cell-count subsampling}

Cell-count robustness was evaluated on all cohorts for which a
sufficient cell count was available (kidney, lung, immune,
immune-invariant, external lung, and Delorey COVID lung) at cell
counts $\{200, 500, 1{,}000, 2{,}000, 3{,}000, 5{,}000, 8{,}000,
15{,}000\}$ (clipped to dataset maximum) with eight independent
subsamples per size. An \emph{anchor ablation} was added to address
the concern that using a 3{,}000-cell reference is circular: each
dataset's MVCC was computed independently against a 3{,}000-cell and
an 8{,}000-cell anchor (plus a 15{,}000-cell anchor for Delorey,
whose cell count permits it). Per-cell, per-anchor top-$k$ Jaccard
and overlap were computed at $k \in \{100, 500, 1{,}000, 5{,}000\}$,
plus top-1\%, top-5\%, and top-10\% proportions of the edge universe,
and per-target top-5 and top-10 overlaps.

The Minimum Valid Cell Count (MVCC) is the smallest cell count at which
the mean top-1{,}000 Jaccard exceeds 0.5 against the reference anchor.
We report MVCC per (dataset, anchor, scorer) triple so that both
anchor-dependence and scorer-dependence can be read directly from
the results. Benjamini--Hochberg correction was
applied to the family of per-(policy, cell-count) $p$-values testing
whether prior-constrained rankings differ from matched random-gene-set
controls.

\subsection{Axis~2: Cluster-resolution sensitivity}

Across all six cohorts (Table~\ref{tab:datasets}), coarse labels
were broad cell classes and fine labels were precomputed Leiden
subclusters enforced as strict refinements of the coarse partition.
Edge scores combined global and per-group maximum absolute Pearson
correlation, $S = 0.6 R_{\text{global}} + 0.4 R_{\text{group\,max}}$,
over the top 600 HVGs; coarse-vs-fine agreement was the bounded-drift
RSS of §2.2. A tiered calibration applied three null families
(global cell-label shuffle, within-coarse constrained shuffle,
degree-preserving bipartite rewire; 49 permutations each), downgrading
the base RSS recommendation (``fine'' $\leq 0.30$, ``hybrid'' $\leq 0.45$,
else ``coarse'') one level per failed null family. RSS weights were
also swept over the full 66-point simplex at step 0.1, an empirical
null was constructed from 1{,}000 rank permutations per cohort, the
HVG count was swept over $\{300, 600, 900, 1{,}200\}$, and the three
normalization schemes from §2.1 were tested (Supplementary Fig.~S6).

\subsection{Axis~3: Rare-cell-type reliability}

Cell types with $\geq 40$ cells were tested under two pipelines: an
adaptive-panel pipeline on three Tabula Sapiens tissues plus external
lung (88 eligible cell types) and a shared-``primary''-panel pipeline
on all five Tabula Sapiens cohorts (39 eligible cell types); Delorey
COVID lung adds nine coarse compartments. Rarity was defined by
within-dataset cell-count quartile. Eight bootstrap replicates at 80\%
subsampling were drawn per cell type and top-$k = 100$ edges scored,
yielding rank stability (Spearman), top-$k$ Jaccard, and
null-separation AUC and tail gap under cell-permutation and
gene-shuffle nulls. The pre-registered primary reliability gate was
stability $\geq 0.75$, Jaccard $\geq 0.35$, AUC $\geq 0.65$, positive
tail gap, and cell count $\geq 80$; these were fixed before a
five-dimensional 5{,}880-point threshold-sensitivity grid was swept
to test the 0\% rare-passage headline.

\subsection{Axis~4: Donor generalization}

Donor generalization was assessed on immune (up to 13 donors at 200
cells/donor, or 8 at 300 cells/donor) and lung (4 donors, 800
cells/donor) using the hematopoiesis $76 \times 108$ panel
(Supplementary Table~S1; 8{,}208 directed edges before gene-universe
restriction). A fixed-holdout design (two holdout donors held constant
to eliminate train-size / holdout-size confounding) sampled training
donor counts $\in \{2, 4, 6, 8, 10\}$ with 20 random splits per count.
The cells-per-donor parameter was swept between 200 and 800. Transfer
was quantified by Spearman $\rho$, Pearson $r$, top-$k$ overlap and
Jaccard at $k \in \{50, 100, 250, 500\}$, sign-flip rate, and mean
absolute rank shift.

\subsection{Axis~5: Integration-method sensitivity}

Harmony~\citep{Korsunsky2019} was applied in PCA space (50 components)
on immune, lung, and kidney using three batch keys (technical batch,
donor, method); scVI~\citep{Gayoso2022} and Scanorama~\citep{Hie2019}
were run as a cross-method comparison. Sensitivity was measured by
rank Spearman, sign-flip rate, mean absolute rank shift, and top-$k$
overlap relative to uncorrected data. External validation used
precision@500 against DoRothEA AB / ABCD / ChIP-seq--backed subsets
and TRRUST with paired swap binomial tests and BH-FDR correction;
perturbation validation used Dixit-derived
tables~\citep{Dixit2016}. A $n_{\text{pcs}} \in \{30, 50, 80\}$ sweep
probed the robustness--performance tradeoff.

\subsection{Scorer-divergence and cross-axis analyses}

To test transferability of fragility conclusions across scorers, all
five scorers were run on the same 800 kidney cells under the
hematopoiesis $76 \times 108$ panel (after restriction to kidney's
gene universe: 75 of 76 TFs, 99 of 108 targets, 7{,}425 directed
edges); pairwise agreement was reported via Spearman $\rho$, top-100
and top-500 Jaccard, and the bounded-drift RSS. A cross-axis fragility
profile then summarised, per (axis, cohort), the severity of the
robustness concern (pass / marginal / fail) under each axis'
calibrated threshold.

\subsection{Adversarial AI-assisted review protocol}

Each axis passed through an iterative AI-assisted review loop that
adversarially challenged the initial analysis under a fixed rubric
(confounded nulls, overfitted hyperparameters, leakage, composite-metric
uninterpretability, threshold sensitivity); corrective experiments were
designed by the agent, executed, and verified before any claim was
updated. The base model, prompt templates, round-by-round logs, and the
27-issue full-census audit under the frozen rubric are in Supplementary
Methods~S2.

\section{Results}

\subsection{Axis~1: The minimum valid cell count is strongly scorer-dependent
and anchor-invariant}

Under scGPT attention, even moderate cell-count reductions reshape
top-ranked edges: at 200 cells on kidney, top-1{,}000 Jaccard against
the 3{,}000-cell anchor is 0.001--0.029, and at 1{,}000 cells it
remains below 15\%, so the scGPT-attention MVCC exceeds 3{,}000 cells.

The threshold is scorer-specific. Under Pearson correlation on 600
HVGs (Figure~\ref{fig:cellcount}A), the MVCC (smallest cell count at
which mean top-1{,}000 Jaccard exceeds 0.5) is \textbf{200 cells for
kidney, lung, immune-invariant, and external lung} and \textbf{500 cells
for primary immune and Delorey COVID lung}. MVCC is
\emph{anchor-invariant} (Figure~\ref{fig:cellcount}B;
Supplementary Fig.~S1): identical values emerge against
3{,}000-, 8{,}000-, and (Delorey only) 15{,}000-cell anchors,
ruling out anchor-threshold circularity. Kidney Jaccard climbs
0.515 (200 cells) $\to$ 0.747 (1{,}000) $\to$ 0.839 (3{,}000) at the
3{,}000-cell anchor; other cohorts follow similar trajectories
(Supplementary Fig.~S5).

Constraining scGPT-attention scoring to DoRothEA TF--target pairs
stabilises kidney rankings dramatically: the two TF-guided policies
hold Jaccard at 0.73--0.76 (200 cells) and 0.86--0.87 (1{,}000 cells)
versus 0.002--0.008 for matched random gene-set controls
(BH-corrected $q \approx 0.017$; Figure~\ref{fig:cellcount}B), and recover
5.5--6.6\% of top-1{,}000 edges with independent DoRothEA support
(versus 0.2--0.3\% for controls). Prior-constrained policies therefore
keep rankings meaningful far below the scGPT-specific MVCC. The
guidance is: \emph{plan for $\geq 3{,}000$ cells under scGPT or use
prior constraints; 200--500 cells suffice under Pearson, MI, and tree
scorers on the cohorts tested}. §3.6 examines scorer-dependence directly.

\subsection{Axis~2: Dual-null calibration pushes every cohort to coarse
or hybrid clustering; the recommendation is robust to weight choice,
gene dimension, normalization, and the addition of a third null family}

Does coarse-versus-fine clustering change the resulting GRN? Observed
bounded-drift RSS at $k = 1{,}000$ is 0.218 (immune-invariant) to
0.402 (lung) (Figure~\ref{fig:resolution}A). A 1{,}000-permutation
empirical null gives mean $\approx 0.79$ and 5th percentile $\approx 0.73$,
placing every cohort 8.8--13.1 standard deviations below the null mean
($p = 0.001$ floor; Supplementary Fig.~S3): every cohort is therefore
significantly more stable than a random coarse-vs-fine pairing.

Dual-null calibration refines the picture. Only kidney, lung, and
immune-invariant clear the global cell-label shuffle ($p = 0.02$);
\emph{no} cohort clears the within-coarse constrained shuffle
($p \geq 0.14$ across all five Tabula Sapiens cohorts). Requiring both
nulls for any ``fine'' or ``hybrid'' recommendation downgrades four
Tabula Sapiens cohorts to \textbf{coarse} and immune-invariant to
\textbf{hybrid} (Figure~\ref{fig:resolution}B); the apparent
fine-stability of immune-invariant and external lung evaporates once
the within-coarse null is applied. Delorey COVID (116{,}313 cells)
has the lowest raw RSS of any cohort (0.173) but fails the
within-coarse null at $p = 0.69$ and therefore also downgrades to
\textbf{hybrid}.

The dual-null recommendation is invariant to weight choice
(66-point simplex sweep at step 0.1, Supplementary Fig.~S2),
HVG count ($n \in \{300, 600, 900, 1{,}200\}$; immune-invariant
flips coarse$\leftrightarrow$hybrid but never to fine), and
normalization scheme (depth-log1p / size-factor-log1p / Pearson
residuals on kidney all give coarse; Supplementary Fig.~S6). A
triple-null calibration adds a permissive degree-preserving bipartite
rewire as a third null: every cohort clears it at the permutation
floor ($p = 0.02$) while still failing within-coarse at $p \geq 0.22$,
so the within-coarse failure reflects genuine over-partitioning rather
than null over-stringency (Supplementary Fig.~S7).

Within-class heterogeneity remains considerable even after coarsening:
T cells in kidney (RSS 0.466) and immune (0.314), erythroid lineage
in immune (0.535) and immune-invariant (0.522), and B-lineage
lymphocytes in kidney (0.597) are markedly more fine-sensitive than
their surrounding compartments (full per-compartment breakdown in the
reproduction package).

\subsection{Axis~3: Rare cell types exhibit a reliability gap that is
robust to 5{,}880 threshold combinations}

Are edges inferred from rare cell types as trustworthy as those from
abundant types? Across 88 cell types in the four adaptive-panel
datasets (three Tabula Sapiens tissues plus external lung), rare types
match abundant types on overall rank ordering (Spearman 0.884 rare vs
0.851 abundant, $p = 0.835$) but are significantly worse on the
decision-relevant top-$k$ overlap measures: Jaccard 0.625 vs 0.731
($p = 4.1 \times 10^{-3}$), null-separation AUC 0.520 vs 0.620
($p = 5.3 \times 10^{-8}$), tail gap $-0.062$ vs $+0.082$
($p = 1.5 \times 10^{-7}$). Under the pre-registered reliability gate
(stability $\geq 0.75$, Jaccard $\geq 0.35$, primary AUC $\geq 0.65$,
positive tail gap, cell count $\geq 80$), \textbf{0 of 22 rare cell
types pass versus 4 of 22 abundant types}
(Figure~\ref{fig:rare}A--B); under a dual-null formulation (minimum
AUC across cell-permutation and gene-shuffle nulls), the gap
attenuates but stays significant (AUC gap 0.018,
$p = 6.8 \times 10^{-4}$).

A 5{,}880-point threshold grid (5-D sweep over stability, Jaccard,
AUC, tail gap, and cell count) confirms the headline is not
threshold-driven: the abundant-$>$-rare gap direction holds at
\emph{100\%} of grid points, the grid-mean gap is $+0.344$ (wider
than $+0.182$ at primary thresholds), and rare pass rate is 0 at
two-thirds of grid points (Supplementary Fig.~S4). Switching to a
shared 36-edge TRRUST-intersected panel preserves the gap direction
on immune and kidney (Spearman 0.93 and 0.87 for null-AUC ordering)
but not lung (0.09); the shared ``primary'' panel
(DoRothEA A/B $\cap$ TRRUST) on all five Tabula Sapiens cohorts also
preserves it (0\% rare / 5.9\% intermediate / 9.1\% abundant;
Supplementary Table~S3). Minimum reliable cell-size estimates under
the adaptive-panel pipeline range from 287 (external lung,
extrapolated) to 16{,}486 (lung, empirical), so some tissues require
very large cell-type samples before mechanistic edges clear the
reliability gate (Figure~\ref{fig:rare}C).

\paragraph{Disease-cohort validation.}
Delorey COVID lung (116{,}313 cells, 27 donors) presents a
qualitatively different failure mode: across nine coarse compartments,
rank stability is high (Spearman 0.85--0.89) and Jaccard reasonable
(0.53--0.63), but null-separation AUC is uniformly weak (0.50--0.64),
so \textbf{no cell type passes the gate in any rarity class} (0 of 3
each). Disease-driven shared expression programmes (COVID-induced
myeloid / fibroblast remodelling) plausibly compress the
observed-vs-null gap; the gate was calibrated on Tabula Sapiens and
may need disease-specific recalibration for heavily perturbed tissues.

\subsection{Axis~4: Donor generalization improves monotonically; the
required donor count depends on cells-per-donor balancing}

Under the fixed-holdout protocol (two holdout donors held constant),
immune-tissue transfer improves monotonically with training-donor count.
At 300 cells/donor in the 20k-cell immune subset, Spearman $\rho$ rises
0.568 $\to$ 0.683 from 2 to 10 training donors and top-100 overlap
rises 0.445 $\to$ 0.540 (Figure~\ref{fig:donor}A). Expanding to the
top-20 donors (long-tail inclusion) lowers all transfer metrics
($\rho = 0.627$ at 16 donors vs 0.683 at 10 in the top-12 panel),
showing that high-quality-donor restrictions paint an optimistic picture.
The 90\%-of-best criterion recommends \textbf{6 training donors}; the
95\%-of-best criterion recommends \textbf{8}. Under looser balancing
(200 cells/donor, 13 eligible donors), the curve shifts and 90\%-of-best
is met at 2 donors --- the requirement is therefore
\emph{cells-per-donor dependent}, and the cells-per-donor policy must
be pre-registered.

Composition matching (3 shared cell classes, 62 cells/donor) lowers
transfer by 16.3\% on Spearman (0.707 $\to$ 0.591) and 10.7\% on
top-100 overlap (0.610 $\to$ 0.545; Figure~\ref{fig:donor}B): a
substantial fraction of apparent donor generalisation reflects
shared cell-type composition. Even under this strictest protocol the
above-null signal remains large (lift@100 of 37.8--44.3$\times$),
indicating real cross-donor regulatory signal beyond composition.

\subsection{Axis~5: Integration method choice reorders rankings
substantially and does not improve perturbation-grounded validity}

Harmony integration produces moderate-to-large rank perturbations on
all three tissues. On the full HVG-pair universe (unconstrained),
Spearman corrected-vs-uncorrected ranges 0.757--0.823 with sign-flip
rates 8.1--12.7\%. Panel-restricted (hematopoiesis $76 \times 108$):
top-500 overlap 65--71\%, top-1{,}000 overlap 72--75\%, i.e.\
29--35\% top-500 displacement (Figure~\ref{fig:integration}A) ---
roughly a third of prioritised edges change depending on whether
batch correction is applied. Yet rankings stay highly non-random:
bounded-drift RSS between baseline and Harmony-integrated lists is
0.26--0.30 across the eight (tissue $\times$ batch-key) combinations
vs null mean $\approx 0.72$ ($|z| \geq 37$; empirical $p$ at floor).

\paragraph{Integration method matters.}
A cross-method comparison (Harmony vs scVI vs Scanorama, same
cells, same panel, same random seed) reveals that method choice is
not neutral (Table~\ref{tab:integration_methods}). Three regimes emerge:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Harmony} preserves rankings reasonably well across all
      three tissues (Spearman 0.63--0.87, top-1{,}000 overlap
      0.65--0.87, sign-flip 6--17\%).
\item \textbf{scVI} sits in the middle: Spearman 0.36--0.57, top-1{,}000
      overlap 0.48--0.74, sign-flip 18--30\%. Roughly a quarter of
      edges change sign compared to the uncorrected baseline.
\item \textbf{Scanorama} produces near-random rankings on kidney and
      lung (Spearman $\approx 0$, top-1{,}000 overlap 0.11--0.14,
      sign-flip 44--47\%; RSS at the rank-permutation null mean of
      $\approx 0.72$) and weakly correlated rankings on immune
      (Spearman 0.10, top-1{,}000 overlap 0.62, sign-flip 26\%).
\end{itemize}

In other words, switching integration method on the same data can
cause anywhere from 6\% to 47\% of the top-500 edges to change sign,
and can move the top-1{,}000 ranking to statistical indistinguishability
from a random rewire of the baseline. Any GRN claim downstream of
integration therefore depends materially on integration-method choice;
we recommend reporting rank-stability metrics against the uncorrected
baseline for every integration tool used.

\paragraph{Do integrated rankings better recover reference edges?}
Raw precision@500 shows nominal Harmony gains on DoRothEA and TRRUST
in immune and kidney, but paired swap tests with BH-FDR correction
restrict robust improvements to TRRUST on immune and kidney
($\Delta$ precision $+0.026$ to $+0.028$, FDR $< 0.05$;
Figure~\ref{fig:integration}B); DoRothEA and ChIP-seq--backed gains
do not survive correction. Perturbation validation against
Perturb-seq~\citep{Dixit2016} returns small, negative top-500 swap
deltas ($-0.002$ to $-0.004$, FDR $= 1.0$): edges \emph{gained} by
integration are no more likely to be experimentally validated than
edges \emph{lost}. Ranking perturbation does not translate into
biological gain even for the best-behaved integrator. A
$n_{\text{pcs}} \in \{30, 50, 80\}$ sweep reveals a tradeoff: top-500
overlap with baseline rises monotonically with PCs (immune
$0.590 \to 0.736$, more conservative integration), but DoRothEA
precision peaks at intermediate PCs (Figure~\ref{fig:integration}C),
so no single setting maximises both stability and reference agreement.

\subsection{Scorer divergence: within-scorer fragility does not transfer across scorer families}

Scoring the hematopoiesis $76 \times 108$ panel on a shared 800-cell
kidney sample with all five scorers reveals the pairwise agreement
landscape (Table~\ref{tab:scorer_divergence},
Figure~\ref{fig:scorer_divergence}). The two tree-ensemble scorers
(GRNBoost2 and GENIE3) are near-identical (Spearman $\rho = 0.93$,
top-500 Jaccard $= 0.54$); Pearson is moderately correlated with the
tree scorers ($\rho \in [0.52, 0.56]$); mutual information is weakly
correlated with Pearson ($\rho = 0.20$) and unrelated to the tree
scorers ($\rho \leq 0.21$). Most importantly, \textbf{scGPT attention
is uncorrelated with every classical scorer}: Spearman $\rho$ ranges
$-0.05$ (vs Pearson) to $+0.20$ (vs MI), top-100 Jaccard 0.02--0.06,
and the RSS composite 0.69--0.76 sits at or above the
rank-permutation null mean ($\approx 0.72$). The Axes 1--5
within-scorer fragility analysis is therefore orthogonal to
across-scorer agreement: choosing a stable scorer does not guarantee
another stable scorer will agree with it, and an analyst switching
scorers must re-audit.

\subsection{Cross-axis analysis}

The five axes are not independent
(Table~\ref{tab:fragility}, Figure~\ref{fig:crossaxis}). Three
interactions matter for practice. First, Axis~1 and Axis~3 are
mechanistically linked under scGPT attention: rare types ($<\!500$
cells) operate below the scGPT MVCC of $\sim$3{,}000, partly
explaining their 0\% reliability-gate passage; under Pearson and tree
scorers (MVCC 200--500) the link is weaker, so the rare-vs-abundant
gap is also driven by sparsity and lower null-separation in small
subsamples. Second, the dual-null coarse recommendation from Axis~2
indirectly aids Axis~4, since coarse clustering pools cell-type
variation that would otherwise require per-donor composition matching.
Third, Axis~5 operates upstream of every other axis by altering the
correlation structure on which scores are computed, so integration
choice is a latent confounder for cell-count, resolution, and rare-type
calibrations. No dataset passes all five axes under their calibrated
criteria in any single scorer family, no single corrective measure
resolves all fragilities, and within-scorer fragility profiles do not
transfer across scorer families: robustness must be demonstrated, not
assumed, along each axis \emph{and} each scorer choice.

% 4. DISCUSSION
% ============================================================
\section{Discussion}

This audit demonstrates that GRN edge rankings from single-cell data are fragile
along multiple axes simultaneously, and that apparent robustness along one axis
(e.g., coarse rank stability for rare types) can mask failure along another
(e.g., null separation). The adversarial-review methodology that we applied to
each axis is itself a contribution: by requiring corrective experiments before
claims are updated, it systematically closes the gap between initial findings and
defensible conclusions.

\paragraph{Practical implications.}
Table~\ref{tab:guidelines} provides concrete guidance: no single
parameter choice can guarantee robust GRN inference; robustness must
be demonstrated along each relevant axis for the specific tissue,
cell count, and analytical choices at hand.

\begin{table*}[ht]
\centering
\caption{Practical guidelines for robust GRN inference from single-cell data.}
\label{tab:guidelines}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lX}
\toprule
\textbf{Decision Point} & \textbf{Guideline} \\
\midrule
\textit{Sample size} &
  Target $\geq 3{,}000$ cells under scGPT attention or use informative
  prior constraints (DoRothEA / TRRUST) which stabilise scGPT rankings
  at 200+ cells. Under Pearson, mutual-information, and tree-based
  scorers, 200--500 cells are often sufficient on the cohorts tested;
  verify per-scorer MVCC before committing. \\
\addlinespace
\textit{Clustering} &
  Default to coarse clustering unless a dual-null calibration (global +
  within-coarse constrained shuffle) confirms that fine resolution
  adds separable signal; in our six cohorts, no coarse-vs-fine
  configuration cleared both nulls, so every recommendation collapses
  to coarse or hybrid. Report within-class RSS for compartment-level
  uncertainty. \\
\addlinespace
\textit{Rare cell types} &
  Report null-separation AUC alongside rank stability for every cell
  type; a high Spearman stability can coexist with null-level AUC
  (Delorey COVID cohort, §3.3). Flag rare types ($< 250$ cells) as
  hypothesis-generating. Provide minimum-reliable-size estimates with
  uncertainty status. \\
\addlinespace
\textit{Donor coverage} &
  Collect $\geq 6$ donors under strict cells-per-donor balancing
  (300 cells/donor, 8 donors for conservative generalisation); under
  looser balancing (200 cells/donor), the requirement drops to 2--4
  donors. State the cells-per-donor policy explicitly. Use
  fixed-holdout evaluation, not complementary splits, and report
  composition-controlled transfer alongside raw transfer. \\
\addlinespace
\textit{Integration} &
  Run at least two integration methods on the same cells, not only the
  tool that happens to be most popular in the pipeline: we observe
  Spearman ranging from 0.06 (Scanorama on kidney) to 0.87 (Harmony
  on immune) on identical inputs. Report at least paired swap
  significance for reference gains, plus one perturbation-backed
  metric when available, and include a hyperparameter-sensitivity
  sweep. Avoid universal claims of biological improvement. \\
\addlinespace
\textit{Scorer choice} &
  Auditing one scorer does not license conclusions about another: in
  our kidney experiment, scGPT-attention rankings overlap with Pearson
  rankings in fewer than 5\% of top-100 edges. Re-audit every axis
  under the scorer used to generate the published rankings. \\
\addlinespace
\textit{Cross-axis audit} &
  Report a fragility profile (Table~\ref{tab:fragility}) covering all
  axes relevant to the study design. Acknowledge which axes were not
  testable and why. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table*}

\paragraph{Comparison to existing benchmarks.}
Published GRN benchmarks~\citep{Pratapa2020, Chen2018} have focused primarily on
accuracy relative to gold-standard references, treating preprocessing as fixed.
Our audit complements these by asking whether rankings are \emph{stable} across
realistic analytical perturbations, a necessary condition for practical utility.
The two perspectives are not redundant: a method can be accurate under one
configuration and fragile under perturbation, or robustly mediocre across all
configurations.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
None of the five scorers directly estimate causal regulatory effects;
correlation on normalized counts avoids zero-inflation
artifacts~\citep{Svensson2020} but remains susceptible to confounding
by shared upstream regulators~\citep{Kharchenko2014}, tree-ensemble
scorers introduce their own biases, and scGPT attention reflects
pretraining patterns on the CellxGene Census rather than causal edges.
The audit covers four Tabula Sapiens tissues plus one external-lab
SmartSeq2 lung cohort and one COVID-19 lung disease cohort;
generalisation to other disease tissues, developmental atlases, and
cross-species comparisons remains untested. The within-coarse null is
demanding and other scoring families might clear it; perturbation-backed
validation remains data-sparse, limiting swap-test power. Three
integration methods are compared side-by-side
(Harmony~\citep{Korsunsky2019}, scVI~\citep{Gayoso2022},
Scanorama~\citep{Hie2019}); broader integration
benchmarks~\citep{Luecken2022, Tran2020} suggest extending the
cross-method comparison further is a natural next step.

% ============================================================
% 5. CONCLUSION
% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}

This systematic multi-scorer, multi-axis robustness audit of
single-cell GRN inference --- five scorers, six cohorts, five axes
(cell count, clustering resolution, rare-cell reliability, donor
generalisation, integration sensitivity) --- finds fragility to be
pervasive, axis-specific, interactive, and scorer-specific. No dataset
passes all five axes under calibrated criteria in any scorer family;
the within-coarse resolution null fails universally under dual- and
triple-null calibrations; the rare-vs-abundant reliability gap holds
across 5{,}880 threshold combinations; and scGPT attention rankings
are statistically uncorrelated with classical-scorer rankings on the
same cells. The accompanying \texttt{fragility} package reproduces
every CSV and figure from raw h5ad via a single \texttt{make audit}
command and is extensible to new scorers and datasets through a single
interface; we encourage practitioners to conduct analogous audits
before publishing GRN claims.
